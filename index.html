About

I'm a computational immunologist and bioinformatics scientist with 8+ years building analytical pipelines for high-dimensional immune data — from flow cytometry to single-cell RNA sequencing to TCR repertoire analysis.

Most recently, I served as a contract Computational Immunology Scientist for Marker Therapeutics, where I architected end-to-end TCR repertoire pipelines for longitudinal clinical trial datasets, integrating immune dynamics with clinical response categories to inform translational decisions.

My work sits at the intersection of immunology, machine learning, and software engineering — building tools (R packages, FlowJo plugins, ML ensembles) that end up in papers, patents, and executive boardrooms.

Based in Hawaii. Open to senior and principal-level roles in translational biotech and computational immunology.

Featured Projects

R Package Open Source

CytoCrunch

A comprehensive R package bridging FlowJo's plugin ecosystem and Bioconductor workflows. CytoCrunch manages, transforms, and extends flow cytometry data using a custom S4 object system, enabling clustering, dimensionality reduction, and scRNA-seq–style analysis on traditional cytometry outputs. Built to handle millions of cells across multi-tissue, longitudinal studies.
